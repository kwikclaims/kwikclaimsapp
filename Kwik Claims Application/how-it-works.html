Our 10‑Step Process

Documentation quality is the foundation. This is the repeatable process we use to turn job photos and site data into carrier‑ready reports and estimates — then keep the claim moving through follow‑up.

Start with Insurance Claim ManagementGet Started

The 10 steps

This is written for contractors, tradesmen, and property teams — simple, repeatable, and focused on approvals.

1) Intake & eligibility check

Capture loss date, address, contact info, and basic coverage context.

2) Job setup in the system

Create a clean file structure and documentation checklist.

3) Guided photo documentation

We tell you what to photograph so nothing important is missed.

4) Measurements & scope mapping

Rooms, elevations, and affected components measured and mapped.

5) Code & compliance review

Local code items and required upgrades identified where applicable.

6) Estimate generation (market pricing)

Line‑item estimate built for your market and your workflow.

7) Narrative report writing

Clear justification: what happened, what’s damaged, and why the scope is required.

8) Package assembly

Photos, measurements, forms, and supporting docs assembled into an insurer‑friendly packet.

9) Submission & carrier coordination

We coordinate with the adjuster and respond to requests.

10) Approval tracking & close‑out

Daily updates, supplements as needed, and guidance through release + completion.

On‑site capture checklist

A few examples of what we typically request (varies by loss type):

  • Wide shots + close-ups of all affected areas
  • Source/cause indicators (where safely accessible)
  • Room‑by‑room documentation
  • Exterior elevations (when applicable)
  • Photos that show line‑item justification (materials, damage patterns)
  • Any prior repairs or pre‑existing conditions (documented clearly)

Why this works

Insurance workflows move faster when the scope is clear, documented, and easy to validate.

Adjuster‑friendly clarity

A clean narrative and photo mapping reduces guesswork.

Fewer missing items

Guided capture and checklists reduce missed scope.

Consistent packaging

Every file looks professional and organized.

Trackable follow‑up

Requests, supplements, and approvals are actively managed.
